## What you can do without asking

- **Refer to the project by name.** Use "Curie" in truthful, descriptive
  statements about the project, such as "compatible with Curie," "built on

  keeping the Curie name that identifies it.
- **Write about Curie** in articles, tutorials, talks, and documentation.
- **State that your product integrates with or supports Curie**, as long as

- Using the Curie logo in a modified form, or using the Marks in a domain

## Modified versions

If you distribute a modified version of Curie, you must not present it as the
official Curie or imply that CurieTech AI endorses it. Give your fork a
distinct name, and you may accurately state that it is "based on Curie" or "a
fork of Curie."

## General guidance

- Do not alter or abbreviate the Marks in ways that create a new mark.
- Do not incorporate the Marks into your own logos or trademarks.
- When in doubt, use the Marks to point to this project, not to brand your own.
